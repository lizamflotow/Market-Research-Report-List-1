<p><p>The uterine stent market features two primary types: plastic and metal stents. Plastic stents, often made from biocompatible materials, are lightweight, flexible, and designed for temporary use, providing effective support during procedures like hysteroscopy. Metal stents, typically composed of stainless steel or other alloys, offer greater strength and durability, making them suitable for longer-term applications. Both types are utilized to maintain the patency of the uterus, addressing various gynecological conditions and improving patient outcomes in minimally invasive surgeries.</p></p>
