<p><p>The phenolic resin market for friction materials comprises two primary types: liquid and powder. Liquid phenolic resol resins are characterized by their quick curing properties, making them ideal for high-performance applications, where they provide excellent adhesion and thermal stability. In contrast, powder phenolic novolac resins require a hardener to cure and are often used for specific applications requiring robust mechanical strength and wear resistance. Both types play crucial roles in manufacturing brake linings, clutches, and other friction components.</p></p>
