<p><p>High-temperature composite materials are advanced materials designed to withstand extreme temperatures while maintaining structural integrity and performance. These materials are typically composed of high-performance fibers, such as carbon or ceramic, embedded in a matrix of polymers, metals, or ceramics. The shift toward sustainable energy solutions, such as wind turbines and nuclear reactors, also contributes to market expansion.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include the development of next-generation ceramic matrix composites (CMCs) and polymer matrix composites (PMCs) with enhanced thermal stability and mechanical properties. Additionally, advancements in manufacturing technologies, such as additive manufacturing and automated fiber placement, are enabling cost-effective production of complex composite structures. The growing focus on reducing carbon emissions and improving fuel efficiency further fuels the adoption of high-temperature composites in various industries.</p></p>

Metal-based composites integrate metal matrices with reinforcing elements, delivering superior thermal conductivity, mechanical strength, and resistance to wear.
