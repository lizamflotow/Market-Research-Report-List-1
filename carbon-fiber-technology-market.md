<p><p>Carbon fiber technology involves the use of carbon fibers, which are lightweight, high-strength materials composed of thin strands of carbon atoms. However, high production costs and raw material availability remain challenges for widespread adoption.</p></p>

<p><p>Carbon fiber technology utilizes high-strength, lightweight fibers made primarily from two precursor materials: PAN (polyacrylonitrile) and Pitch. PAN-based carbon fibers dominate the market due to their superior tensile strength, versatility, and widespread use in aerospace, automotive, and sports industries. Pitch-based fibers, derived from petroleum or coal tar, excel in thermal conductivity and stiffness, making them ideal for specialized applications like thermal management and industrial components. Both markets cater to diverse industries, with PAN focusing on performance and Pitch on niche, high-performance applications.</p></p>
