<p><p>The cervical cancer diagnostic tests market includes various methods to detect and diagnose cervical cancer. Pap smear tests involve collecting cervical cells to identify abnormalities. Colposcopy uses a magnifying instrument to examine the cervix closely after abnormal results. HPV testing detects the presence of human papillomavirus, a key cause of cervical cancer. Endocervical curettage (ECC) involves scraping the cervical canal for abnormal cells, while cone biopsy removes a cone-shaped section of cervical tissue for further analysis. Other methods may include imaging and molecular testing.</p></p>
