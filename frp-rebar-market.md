<p><p>FRP (Fiber-Reinforced Polymer) Rebar is a composite material made of fibers, such as glass, carbon, or basalt, embedded in a polymer resin matrix. It is used as a lightweight, corrosion-resistant alternative to traditional steel rebar in construction, particularly in environments prone to corrosion, such as marine structures, bridges, and roads. FRP Rebar offers high tensile strength, durability, and resistance to chemicals, making it an attractive choice for modern infrastructure projects.</p><p>The FRP Rebar market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for sustainable and long-lasting construction materials.
