<p><p>The industrial-grade hydrogel market is primarily divided into two categories: natural and synthetic hydrogels. Natural hydrogels are derived from biological sources like plant and animal tissues, offering biocompatibility and biodegradability, making them suitable for applications in tissue engineering and drug delivery. Synthetic hydrogels, on the other hand, are chemically engineered to achieve specific properties, granting enhanced control over their physical and chemical characteristics. They are commonly used in wound dressings, agriculture, and absorbent products due to their versatility and durability.</p></p>
