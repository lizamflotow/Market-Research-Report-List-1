<p><ul><li>Invasive ICP Devices</li><li>Non-invasive ICP Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Intracranial Pressure (ICP) monitoring devices market is divided into invasive and non-invasive categories. Invasive ICP devices, such as ventricular catheters and fiber optic sensors, are inserted into the cranial cavity to provide direct pressure measurements, offering precise data but carrying risks of infection and complications. Non-invasive ICP devices, including transcranial Doppler ultrasound and optical methods, estimate intracranial pressure without surgical procedures, making them safer and more patient-friendly. The choice between them depends on clinical needs and risk assessments.</p></p>
