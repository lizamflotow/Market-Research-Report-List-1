<p><p>Wireless temperature monitoring technologies vary in communication methods. The 900 MHz RF utilizes low-frequency signals for long-range, low-power applications, making it ideal for various environments. Digital Cat5 cable systems offer reliable wired connections, typically used in controlled settings. WiFi 802.11 B/G/N and 802.11 G operate on higher frequencies, providing robust connectivity and higher data rates. These WiFi standards are suitable for applications requiring real-time monitoring and integration with existing wireless networks, enhancing flexibility and scalability in temperature management.</p></p>
