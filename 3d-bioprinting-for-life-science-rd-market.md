<p><p>3D bioprinting in life science R&D encompasses various techniques, each serving distinct applications. Magnetic 3D bioprinting utilizes magnetic fields to assembly cellular structures, enhancing tissue functionality. Laser-assisted bioprinting employs focused lasers to precisely deposit biomaterials for creating complex scaffolds. Inkjet 3D bioprinting leverages droplet ejection to layer bioinks, offering versatility in structure design. Microextrusion 3D bioprinting uses continuous filament extrusion, enabling the fabrication of large tissue constructs. Together, these methods advance tissue engineering and regenerative medicine research.</p></p>
