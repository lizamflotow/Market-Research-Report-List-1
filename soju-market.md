Distilled Soju is produced through traditional fermentation and distillation processes, resulting in a higher alcohol content and a richer flavor profile. In contrast, diluted Soju is achieved by mixing distilled Soju with water or other ingredients, which lowers the alcohol content and creates a smoother taste. This segmentation caters to varying consumer preferences, with distilled Soju appealing to traditionalists and diluted varieties attracting casual drinkers.</p></p>
